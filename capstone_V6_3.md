## 1. Purpose and Scope

This is a portfolio-grade probabilistic forecasting project built for an industry Data Scientist hiring audience. The tool produces, on demand, an hourly forecast of the **German–Luxembourg (DE-LU) bidding-zone day-ahead electricity price** for the **next delivery day (24 hourly values, D+1)**, with calibrated 50% / 80% / 95% prediction intervals.

The target is the next delivery day specifically because that is what the day-ahead market clears: the EPEX SPOT day-ahead auction (within the EU Single Day-Ahead Coupling, cleared by EUPHEMIA) closes its order book at **12:00 CET on D-1, with preliminary results ~12:45 CET and final results ~12:57 CET**. At that gate the prices for all hours of D+1 are forecast, and every lagged-price feature is legitimately known. A multi-day-ahead target would force the model to consume values that do not yet exist at forecast time — look-ahead leakage in the one project whose centerpiece is a leakage audit. The 24h horizon keeps the feature catalog clean and the methodology airtight. (See Section 5.2.)

*Why the German market, for an Israeli interviewer:* the domain is a methodology vehicle, not a domain commitment — a real, noisy, public time-series with a textbook regime shift (the 2021–2023 European energy crisis), the world's most-studied negative-price phenomenon, and a published benchmark literature (EPEX-DE is one of the five canonical Lago et al. 2021 open-access markets), chosen so the work is verifiable rather than a toy. The methodology transfers to any consequential time-series. The full interview answer is prepared in interview prep, not here.

The constraints, named up front:

- **Interactive marimo showcase, statically deployed.** A reactive marimo notebook (server mode, not WASM) with live UI sliders **drawn from the strict-gate champion's actually-retained features** (**v6.3:** the quantile-level selector is fixed; the remaining controls are set at CP-4 from the retained champion catalog, not pre-ratified — §9.2), containerized via a multi-stage Dockerfile and deployed to Hugging Face Spaces free tier. Data is a fixed weekly snapshot manually refreshed during the active application phase, not live API pulls during user sessions. **(v6.2)** The recruiter-facing **primary URL** is a statically exported HTML report of the full reading order on **GitHub Pages** — no container, no sleep state; the interactive Space is the deep-dive linked from it with an explicit wake label (Section 9.2).
- **Cloud-backed experiment tracking via DagsHub-hosted MLflow.** Public read-only MLflow UI linked from CV and LinkedIn. No DVC. Data reproducibility achieved via a single committed Parquet snapshot (CC BY 4.0 with attribution) plus pinned dependencies plus tagged commit.
- **Single model.** A monolithic LightGBM quantile ensemble (nine native quantile heads). No model menagerie, no neural challenger, no blending, no shadow logging.
- **CQR-calibrated quantile intervals.** Conformalized Quantile Regression (Romano et al. 2019) on the four symmetric quantile pairs, with isotonic monotonicity enforced last. Per-interval finite-sample marginal coverage **attaches to the post-CQR/pre-isotonic output; the final post-isotonic intervals are verified empirically** (**v6.3** three-stage coverage — §6.2, §8.4). No sequential conformal apparatus (EnbPI / SPCI) and no Giacomini-White gating.
- **Forecast + diagnostic report, not a trading product.** No LONG/SHORT/FLAT layer, no battery-arbitrage optimization, no bid-reference logic, no utility/risk-aversion parameter, no auto-generated natural-language commentary.

The audience is a Data Science hiring manager. The artifact must demonstrate: domain understanding, defensible feature engineering, correct time-series validation, sensible model choice, explainability, cloud-backed experiment tracking via a publicly inspectable MLflow UI, honest uncertainty communication, and a polished interactive interface deployed to a recognizable ML hosting target. Total compute footprint: trains end-to-end in minutes on an Apple Silicon M3, no GPU required. Total hosting cost: $0 (Hugging Face Spaces free tier + DagsHub free tier).

## 2. The DE-LU Day-Ahead Market: Context and Regime Shifts

DE-LU is the German–Luxembourg bidding zone (split from DE-AT-LU on **2018-10-01**; the project's 2019-01-01 window start sits cleanly inside the continuous DE-LU series). The day-ahead price clears in the EPEX SPOT auction within the EU Single Day-Ahead Coupling (SDAC), solved by the EUPHEMIA algorithm: order book closes **12:00 CET D-1**, results publish ~12:45–12:57 CET. Harmonized clearing-price bounds are **+4,000 / −500 EUR/MWh**; the cap auto-increases by +1,000 EUR/MWh after near-cap events (it rose +3,000 → +4,000 effective 2022-05-10, triggered by French hours of €2,712.99 and €2,987.78 on 2022-04-03; a further step to +5,000 was announced and then frozen). **The floor moves to −600 EUR/MWh from 2026-05-28** — a data note for the snapshot era, since the deployed demo's weekly snapshots will straddle that change.

The model must absorb a **three-regime non-stationarity**. These are not historical curiosities — they are the live structure of current pricing behavior.

### 2.1 The 2021–2023 energy crisis and its normalization

German day-ahead prices averaged ~€30/MWh across 2015–2020, then ~€97 in 2021, **~€235 in 2022** — with the **August 2022 monthly average near €469/MWh** as gas-fired plants set the marginal price in the vast majority of hours (day-ahead price vs. gas price correlation ~93% through the crisis; TTF peaked at €339/MWh on 2022-08-26). Normalization followed: €95.18 (2023), €78.51 (2024), €89.32 (2025, Bundesnetzagentur). The crisis is an exogenous structural break of textbook clarity — it ran for ~18 months and is cleaner than any single-storm event.

The model carries two explicit regime features encoding this timeline: `crisis_period` (binary, 2021-09-01 ≤ t ≤ 2022-12-31 — the gas run-up through the peak year; the invasion date 2022-02-24 is the hard exogenous anchor inside it) and `post_crisis` (binary, t ≥ 2023-01-01). Together they encode the pre-crisis / crisis / post-crisis partition. Both are first-class features (Section 4), and the Month-1 change-point block validates the boundaries empirically rather than assuming them — the detector should land near the autumn-2021 run-up and/or the early-2022 shock, and near the late-2022 normalization.

### 2.2 Solar build-out and the negative-price era

The post-crisis regime is not a return to 2019. German PV capacity reached ~100 GW by early 2026 (wind ~73 GW); the last three nuclear plants closed **2023-04-15** (treated as a secondary covariate, not a hard regime boundary — the crisis dominates the price record). The structural consequence is **solar cannibalization and a negative-price explosion**: official Bundesnetzagentur counts of negative day-ahead hours run 139 (2021) → 69 (2022, crisis-suppressed) → 301 (2023) → 457 (2024) → **573 (2025, a record)**, with a single hour at the **−500 floor in 2023** and a 36-consecutive-hour negative run the same year. The mechanism — high renewable infeed meeting must-run and subsidized inflexible generation — is reinforced by market-premium rules that historically paid producers through negative hours (the rule has tightened stepwise; the Solarspitzengesetz era continues that direction). Scarcity has its own modern face: **Dunkelflaute** (low wind + low solar + high load), e.g., the December 2024 / January 2025 events that pushed day-ahead hours toward €583/MWh and triggered a Bundeskartellamt probe (which found no abuse).

**Implication for the model.** The price distribution is non-stationary in three regimes with two distinct stress directions — high-price scarcity (crisis, Dunkelflaute) and negative-price abundance (solar middays). The system therefore mandates walk-forward CV spanning all three regimes with both stress directions represented in eval folds, explicit regime indicator features, and per-regime calibration checks — all covered below. The 2019→pull-date training window (Section 3) spans the full arc while keeping the pre-crisis era from dominating the data the forecast actually serves.

## 4. Feature Catalog

Approximately 25 features, grouped by source. Every feature is classified as either **known-at-forecast-time (KFT)** — publicly available before the 12:00 CET D-1 gate — or **lagged-only (LAG)**. This distinction is the foundation of the leakage audit in Section 5.2. **v6.3:** the DA VRE forecast (A69) and everything derived from it are **neither KFT nor LAG-eligible for the champion** — A69 is published *after* the gate (§3, §5.2), so it is **excluded from the champion schema** and appears only in the §7.2 post-gate benchmark; the champion substitutes gate-feasible proxies **where the M2/CP-2 ablation retains them** (§4.1), else ships on the strict-gate base. Because the target is the next delivery day (D+1, Section 1), every LAG feature below is legitimately available at forecast time: the most recent cleared DA price is for day `D0`, and a 24h lag of a `D0+1` target lands on `D0`.

### 4.0 Time indexing, DST, and the 15-minute transition (mandatory)

The entire pipeline is indexed on **UTC**. `entsoe-py` returns tz-aware `Europe/Berlin` timestamps; convert to UTC on ingest and compute all lags in UTC so 24h = 24 rows, 168h = 168 rows, always. The spring-forward day carries **23 local hours** and the fall-back day **25** (a repeated local hour) — hours are never dropped or duplicated ad hoc; the user-facing "24-value D+1 vector" renders as 23 or 25 values on the two DST-transition days, published as UTC-derived local hours with an explicit DST note. **15-minute MTU boundary (2025-10-01):** post-transition quarter-hours are averaged to hourly per §0 item 4; the hourly series must be continuous across the boundary with the post-transition hourly value equal to the mean of its four quarter-hours (**spike-validated on samples to 1e-9**).

**Spike-established (2026-06-12):** the load and VRE feeds are **natively PT15M across the whole 2019→present archive** — the quarter-hour→hour mean aggregation applies to the feature feeds **uniformly from day one**; only the price needs date-conditional handling. **Chunk-boundary bin integrity (mandatory):** the yearly chunked pulls produce partial hourly bins where a pull window starts or ends mid-hour on a PT15M series; ingestion must complete bins across chunk edges or drop-and-recover them, and an assertion guards that every aggregated hourly value is built from exactly four quarter-hours (extended invariant test 4, Section 9.4). Left unhandled, this silently loses ~1 hour per chunk boundary. All calendar and aggregation invariants are guarded by tests (Section 9.4) — they correct the integer-lag corruption that otherwise silently hits ~2 shoulder-season days per year plus every PT15M row.

**Calendar (KFT)** — hour-of-day, day-of-week, month, **German federal holiday flag** (the `holidays` package "DE" federal subset — the standard simplification in German EPF papers; state holidays out of scope), `is_day_after_holiday`, **`is_bridge_day`** (Brückentag), 3-level day-type {weekday, Saturday, Sunday/holiday}, daylight-saving transition flag, summer-peak / winter-peak season flags.

**Load forecast (KFT)** — ENTSO-E DA total load forecast for DE-LU (A65/A01; published ≥2h pre-gate by regulation, spike-confirmed). Same-hour-tomorrow value and rolling 24h forecast level.

**VRE forecasts (post-gate — NOT KFT; benchmark-only, v6.3)** — DA wind-onshore, wind-offshore, and solar generation forecasts (A69/A01). Post-gate at the 12:00 gate (§3, §5.2), therefore **excluded from the champion**; used only inside the §7 post-gate information benchmark and its residual-load composite.

**Residual load — two constructions (v6.3).** The delivery-day-A69-based `residual_load_fc(d,h) = load_fc(d,h) − wind_onshore_fc(d,h) − wind_offshore_fc(d,h) − solar_fc(d,h)` is **post-gate → benchmark-only**. For the **champion**, a **gate-feasible residual-load proxy** substitutes the single pinned seasonal-diurnal VRE expectation defined in Section 4.1 — a *candidate* feature whose value is decided by matched ablation at M2/CP-2, **not** an assumed headline. This is the literature-standard German merit-order driver in its gate-feasible form.

**Lagged price (LAG)** — lagged DA price at lag-24h, lag-48h, lag-168h.

**Rolling price statistics (LAG)** — 7-day and 30-day rolling mean and standard deviation of the DA price; 168h rolling 5/50/95 quantiles; **7-day rolling count of negative-price hours** (the DE-specific abundance-regime memory feature). **All rolling windows use a strict `t−1` right edge and are closed-left**, so the current target is never inside its own feature window (leakage guard, Section 5.2).

**Regime indicators (KFT)** — `crisis_period` (2021-09-01 → 2022-12-31) and `post_crisis` (≥ 2023-01-01) static-date binaries (validated, not assumed — Month-1 change-point block); **`dunkelflaute_flag` (post-gate — benchmark-only or evaluation stratum, v6.3)** — the derived scarcity flag: flag = 1 when the forecast VRE share (`(wind_on + wind_off + solar)_fc / load_fc`) falls below a low train-set quantile **and** the load forecast exceeds its train-set median; thresholds are **train-only, refit per fold**. **Because it consumes the A69 VRE forecast it is post-gate → NOT KFT → excluded from the champion.** It survives only as a §7 benchmark feature or a post-hoc regime-stratified evaluation stratum (§8.3) — never a claimed gate-known champion feature. Its gate-feasible **candidate** counterpart is **`expected_scarcity_proxy`** (§4.1), included in the champion only if retained at M2/CP-2 and honestly named because a pre-gate proxy cannot identify tomorrow's *actual* Dunkelflaute. There is no official timestamped Dunkelflaute alert (the v5.5 R-4 alert-sourcing risk class dies with the market switch); mining TSO/BNetzA scarcity notices is named as a stretch goal, not scope.

**EUA carbon (LAG, optional)** — daily EUA close at `t−1` relative to the delivery day, broadcast across the 24 hours; wired only if the Ember/ICAP pull is trivially clean at M1.

### 4.1 Domain-insight candidates (gate-feasible; headline decided at M2/CP-2) — v6.3

**v6.3 framing.** These are strict-gate *candidate* features, not a pre-ratified headline. Both arms share **one deterministic VRE-expectation construction** fixed here before results; there is no hidden climatology-vs.-lagged-vintage choice. Each is retained only if it earns its place by matched ablation at M2/CP-2 (selection rule below).

**Pinned gate-feasible VRE expectation (shared by both arms).** For each fold, aggregate historical A75 actual generation for wind-onshore + wind-offshore + solar on the **proper-training partition only** (never the held-out calibration slice and never eval), and fit the seasonal-diurnal OLS model

> `vre_exp(d,h) = δ0 + Σ_{k=1..3}[c_k sin(2πk·DOY/365.25) + d_k cos(2πk·DOY/365.25)] + Σ_{j=1..23}η_j·1[hour=j] + Σ_{k=1..2}Σ_{u∈{sin,cos}}Σ_{v∈{sin,cos}}ρ_{k,u,v}·u(2πk·DOY/365.25)·v(2πh/24)`

This is a fixed **38-coefficient** basis: intercept, six annual-harmonic terms, 23 reference-coded hour dummies (hour 0 omitted), and eight interactions between the first two annual harmonic pairs and the first diurnal sine/cosine pair. The hourly aggregation follows §4.0. The fit is re-estimated per fold; rows with missing or incomplete A75 VRE are excluded and their count is logged. At inference, `vre_exp(d,h)` consumes **only known calendar coordinates and the frozen coefficient vector** — no live feed, no delivery-day A69, and no actual value. Historical A75 is therefore a **fit target, not a model feature**. A lagged-A69/vintage proxy is outside the frozen four-catalog experiment and belongs only in future work. The VRE-expectation coefficients are persisted in every candidate artifact and in the champion if **either** proxy arm wins.

The gate-feasible residual arm contains exactly one feature:

> `gate_feasible_residual_load_proxy(d,h) = load_fc(d,h) − vre_exp(d,h)`

No second residual-seasonal-normal feature is fitted. With the same seasonal-diurnal OLS basis used for `vre_exp`, subtracting a second fitted normal from this proxy would algebraically cancel the VRE projection and reduce to de-seasonalized `load_fc`; labeling that result residual-load signal would be misleading. The one-feature arm preserves the intended gate-feasible merit-order proxy directly and removes a redundant preprocessing layer.

**Naming (v6.3, disambiguated):** `gate_feasible_residual_load_proxy` is the **champion candidate** built on a pre-gate VRE expectation; the A69-based `residual_load_fc` and its A69-derived diagnostics are **§7.2-benchmark-only** and never enter the champion.

A **secondary candidate**, `expected_scarcity_proxy`, encodes the joint scarcity condition as a gate-feasible composite:

> `expected_scarcity_proxy = relu(load_z) · relu(−vre_exp_z)`

where `load_z` is the gate-available load forecast and `vre_exp_z` is the **pinned §4.1 VRE expectation**, both standardized on **proper-training statistics only** (refit per fold). The product fires only when load is *above* its mean **and** seasonally expected renewables are *below* theirs — the *expected* scarcity corner. Honestly named: it cannot identify tomorrow's *actual* Dunkelflaute, only expected scarcity from calendar-known information. Its interview defense (why `relu`, why a product — a ratio cannot encode an AND condition; a product can) carries from v5.5 and is rehearsed if the candidate is retained.

**Selection rule (v6.3 — matched ablation at M2/CP-2, not SHAP).** The strict-gate candidates enter from M1. Their value is decided at **M2/CP-2 by exactly four pre-specified matched catalogs — (1) strict base; (2) base + the one-feature residual arm {`gate_feasible_residual_load_proxy`}; (3) base + `expected_scarcity_proxy`; (4) base + both arms** — run on the pinned folds with everything else identical (two arms that pass individually may be redundant or harmful together, so the 2×2 is mandatory). **Retention rule, fixed before any results exist:** primary metric = **pooled-across-folds mean pinball loss**; an augmented catalog is eligible only if it (i) **improves pooled mean pinball loss by ≥ 1% relative** over strict base, (ii) does **not degrade raw-head empirical coverage at the 80% level by more than 2 pp** (raw-head, pooled — CQR/isotonic calibration does not exist until M3, §6.2), and (iii) is **stable** — the pinball improvement holds with the same sign on **≥ 4 of 5 folds**. **Deterministic winner:** among eligible augmented catalogs, choose the lowest pooled mean pinball loss; catalogs within 0.1% relative of the lowest loss are treated as tied and resolved by **fewer added features**, then by smaller absolute raw-head calibration error `|coverage_80 − 0.80|`, then by the fixed order **residual arm → scarcity arm → both**. If no augmented catalog is eligible, strict base wins. The winning strict-gate catalog is **frozen at CP-2 before M3.** **SHAP at M3 explains the retained model and may name a headline among already-retained features — it is not the incremental-value test.** **"No catalog clears" is a fully acceptable outcome:** the champion then ships as **strict base — no residual-load or scarcity proxy, no forced headline domain feature.** `forecast_uncertainty_proxy` is **not part of the frozen experiment** — it is named **future work** (§13), not a fifth catalog. `gas_to_load_ratio` stays dead and is not resurrected in any form.

### 4.2 Spectral EDA (dominant-frequency confirmation) — merged at v6.2

Before freezing the calendar/seasonal feature catalog and the regime-stratified reporting plan, we confirm the **price series'** dominant periodicities empirically. We compute a **Welch power-spectral-density estimate** of the detrended, log-untransformed hourly DE-LU price series (Hann window, 50% overlap, `nperseg` a power of two ≥ 2×168 so the daily and weekly bins are both resolved) and label the peaks at f = 1/24 h⁻¹ (daily), 1/168 h⁻¹ (weekly), and 1/12 h⁻¹ (half-day harmonic). These confirm the calendar features (hour-of-day, day-of-week) and motivate testing seasonal candidate constructions; because the periodogram is computed on price (A44), it **does not validate a residual-load proxy or justify retaining it** — that decision belongs exclusively to the M2/CP-2 ablation. The same frequency triad is documented for European day-ahead prices (Weron 2014; Lago et al. 2018). To show that the spectrum is **non-stationary across regimes** — diagnostic evidence that performance must be evaluated across the change-point-defined regimes (§5.1) — we compute per-regime Welch periodograms (pre-crisis 2019–2021; crisis 2021–2022; normalization late-2022→) and note the elevated broadband floor and altered seasonal amplitude in the crisis regime (the crisis-era variance and negative-price spikes inflating the floor is a *feature* of the per-regime plot, not a defect to correct). An ACF plot with reference lines at lags 24h and 168h serves as the time-domain cross-check; an MSTL decomposition (periods 24, 168) is optional.

**Implementation:** module `spectral_eda.py` + thin rendering notebook; detrend/difference before the periodogram so low-frequency trend does not swamp the peaks; irregular timestamps/DST are already resolved upstream by the §4.0 UTC discipline. **Committed artifacts (three mandatory, one optional):** `fig_welch_periodogram.png` (labeled 24h/168h/12h peaks), `fig_per_regime_periodogram.png` (three overlaid curves), `fig_acf_24_168.png`, optional `fig_mstl_decomposition.png`. Acceptance: runs end-to-end on the ingested series in <60s on the M3/16GB CPU stack; `annotate_peaks` returns rows within one FFT bin of 1/24 and 1/168 h⁻¹; no dependency beyond scipy/statsmodels/pandas/matplotlib. A 3–4 sentence interpretation paragraph is written into the project README linking price peaks → calendar structure, treating the residual-load proxy as an ablation candidate rather than a spectral conclusion, and linking per-regime spectral change → regime-stratified, multi-regime evaluation.

**Scope guardrails (defended boundary, same class as §13):** descriptive analysis only. No filter design, no wavelet/EMD work, no forecasting in the frequency domain, and no spectral features in the operative v6.3 model catalog. The periodogram justifies the measured calendar periodicities and motivates regime-stratified diagnostics; it does not determine fold boundaries; it proves no causal claim — the README paragraph says "confirms/justifies," never "proves." Budget 6–10h inside M1's window; overrun escalates per R-5 (§11). ## 5. Methodology: Walk-Forward CV and Data Leakage Audit

The two methodology choices below are the top sources of overstated results in the EPF literature (Lago et al. 2021) and the top questions a DS interviewer asks about a time-series project. Both are spelled out explicitly so they live in the final report.

### 5.1 Walk-forward expanding-window CV with 24h embargo — pinned three-regime scheme

Validation uses **expanding-window walk-forward CV with five folds**, each with a **24-hour embargo gap** and a **90-day eval block**, anchored so the eval blocks span **all three regimes and both stress directions** (≥2 pre-crisis, 1 crisis-peak, ≥2 post-crisis including a negative-price-stress block). Concentrating the blocks in the recent tail would make the regime-stratified table (Section 8.3) un-fillable from eval folds and would violate the EPF principle of testing across regimes.

**Pinned scheme** (training window 2019-01-01 → the M1 pull date; each fold trains on all data up to its eval start minus the 24h embargo; expanding — each training set is a superset of the prior):

| Fold | Eval block | Regime |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2020-07-01 → 2020-09-28 | pre-crisis (COVID-recovery summer — flagged as atypical-demand in the stratified read; training span ~1.5y, the thinnest fold by design) |
| 2 | 2021-04-01 → 2021-06-29 | pre-crisis — the last clean window before the autumn-2021 gas run-up |
| 3 | 2022-07-01 → 2022-09-28 | **crisis peak** — contains the ~€469/MWh August-2022 month; the high-price stress block |
| 4 | 2025-05-01 → 2025-07-29 | post-crisis; **solar/negative-price stress block** — the record-negative-hours summer (573 negative hours in 2025, summer-clustered) |
| 5 | final 90 days ending at the most-recent available data at the M1 pull (dates pinned at the pull) | post-crisis current tail; **fully post-15-min-transition** — the headline eval is deliberately evaluated on the quarter-hour-averaged hourly series the deployed product actually serves |

The regime-stratified error table is populated from eval folds for all three regimes. Finer stress rows (e.g., the December-2024 Dunkelflaute week) are sourced from **training-period backtesting** — re-predicting in-window days with a model trained only on data prior to those days — standard, and not an out-of-sample claim.

**Recalibration cadence (carried from v5.5).** At each fold boundary, re-fit the LightGBM heads and CQR thresholds; re-fit the shared VRE climatology and proxy standardizations **only for catalogs that contain those candidates and, after CP-2, only if retained by the champion**; for the §7.2 benchmark only, also re-fit the A69-derived Dunkelflaute/VRE-share thresholds. Within a 90-day block, predict D+1 sequentially without peeking. Per-day recalibration is out of scope under the $0 compute ceiling; per-fold recalibration is the stated lean substitute (§0 item 1).

**Why the 24h embargo suffices given 48h/168h lags (verbatim reasoning, market-relabeled).** The embargo's only job is to break the autocorrelation adjacency between the **last training target** and the **first validation feature**. A 168h-lagged feature of a validation row reaches back into the training period — but using a known, already-cleared historical price as an input feature is not leakage; it is exactly what the production model has at the 12:00 CET gate. A 24h embargo (one full target horizon) removes the only true adjacency. Document this reasoning verbatim for the interviewer.

### 5.2 Data leakage audit

Every feature in Section 4 is classified KFT or LAG with explicit justification. The D+1 target makes the LAG features legitimate: no LAG feature consumes a value dated on or after the delivery day.

- **KFT features** confirmed available before the 12:00 CET D-1 gate: the DA load forecast (published ≥2h pre-gate **by regulation**, Art. 6(2)(b) of Reg. 543/2013, **and empirically present at a 15:35 CEST D-1 spike pull**), calendar features (trivially known), regime binaries (static date thresholds), and the **gate-feasible candidates** (`gate_feasible_residual_load_proxy` and `expected_scarcity_proxy`) built only from pre-gate inputs (§4.1). **v6.3:** `dunkelflaute_flag` is **removed from the champion's KFT set** — it consumes the post-gate A69 forecast, so it is not gate-known (see the VRE bullet).
- **VRE forecasts — the strict-gate resolution (R-2), v6.3.** Observed 2026-06-12: the TP's A69/A01 for delivery day D is **not yet published at 15:35 CEST D-1** — initial publication lands in the 15:35–18:00 window, *after* the 12:00 gate. **v6.3 resolves this by construction, not by convention:** delivery-day A69 is **post-gate → not KFT → excluded from the champion runtime schema.** The champion carries only gate-feasible runtime features — the proxies (§4.1) built from the KFT load forecast, calendar coordinates, and frozen proper-training-only A75 climatology parameters **where the M2/CP-2 ablation retains them**, else the strict-gate base alone. Delivery-day A69 and features computed from it enter **only** the explicitly-labeled **post-gate information benchmark** (§7.2), which quantifies what post-gate information would be worth but never ships. There is **no lagged-vintage implementation choice** inside the four catalogs: a lagged-A69 alternative is future work. The archive-overwrite harness (first snapshot 2026-06-12 for delivery 2026-06-13) remains benchmark provenance evidence; the champion's historical-VRE lineage is instead the explicit proper-training-only A75 fit in §4.1. Revision metadata (`revisionNumber`, `createdDateTime`) is a confirmed dead end (constant / equal to pull wall-clock). The strict-gate posture is a stronger interview asset than as-archived honesty: the shipped model is feasible at 12:00 by construction.
- **LAG features** use `t−1` or earlier relative to the forecast origin `D0`. Rolling statistics (including the negative-hour count) use a strict `t−1` right edge, closed-left.
- **Three audited vectors:** (i) rolling-quantile and rolling-count features computed closed-left so the target is never in its own window; (ii) the VRE-forecast vintage (above); (iii) `crisis_period`/`post_crisis` leak nothing as constants but are collinear with time inside any single-regime fold — a further reason for the three-regime scheme (Section 5.1).
- **Champion/benchmark schema separation (v6.3, replaces the forecast-not-actual guard).** Two enforced **runtime** feature schemas. The **champion runtime schema forbids delivery-day A69, every feature computed from that post-gate vintage, and every actual-value column (A65/A16, A74, A75)**. The only actual-data use is outside the runtime schema: aggregate historical A75 VRE is the §4.1 proper-training-only climatology fit target, and only its frozen coefficients may enter a retained proxy's preprocessing. The **post-gate benchmark runtime schema** explicitly permits delivery-day A69 and its derivatives and nothing else new. The invariant asserts runtime separation plus preprocessing lineage — champion prediction accepts no A69/actual column; the A75 fit uses proper-training rows only; and the benchmark differs from the frozen strict-gate runtime catalog only by delivery-day A69 and its derivatives (§9.4). This subsumes v5.5's forecast-not-actual guard while making the fit-target/runtime distinction explicit.
- **Target transform.** Do **not** apply log/Box-Cox to the target — the DE-LU DA price is *routinely* negative (573 hours in 2025) and has hit the −500 floor. Pinball loss, the CQR score, the additive conformal shift, and monotone rearrangement are all location-equivariant and behave correctly with negative targets and zero-crossing intervals (Section 6). If variance stabilization is ever wanted, use `asinh` (defined on ℝ); with LightGBM quantile heads no transform is needed. In this market the no-transform line is not an edge-case caveat — it is a demonstrated capability.

This audit is the single best preparation against the standard interview question *"How did you make sure your features weren't leaking?"*

## 6. Model: LightGBM Quantile Ensemble with CQR Calibration

*(This section transfers from v5.5 essentially verbatim — it is the methodological core the conversion deliberately preserves.)*

### 6.1 Single monolithic LightGBM, packaged as one model

The target is the hourly DE-LU day-ahead price for the next delivery day. The model is a **single LightGBM regressor family** predicting the nine native quantiles `{p2.5, p5, p10, p25, p50, p75, p90, p95, p97.5}` — one head per level, each fit independently with `objective='quantile'`.

**The champion is one artifact, not nine.** The nine quantile boosters, **only the preprocessing required by the frozen catalog** (the shared VRE-expectation coefficients if either proxy arm is retained; the relevant proper-training standardizations), the **CQR thresholds** (Section 6.2), the final isotonic step, and the feature pipeline are wrapped in a single custom `mlflow.pyfunc.PythonModel` exposing one `predict` that returns the nine calibrated, monotone quantiles. No delivery-day A69 or actual-value column enters `predict` (§5.2/§9.4). This is what is logged, registered, tagged `Production`, and loaded — one model, one Production tag, one `models:/delu_forecast/Production` load path. **The §7.2 post-gate benchmark is a separate, explicitly-labeled artifact with its own runtime schema and its own separate calibration — never registered as the champion.**

### 6.2 CQR calibration, and the post-processing order

LightGBM's native quantile heads have no finite-sample coverage guarantee, and independently fit heads can cross. Both are handled in order:

1. **Raw LightGBM** produces nine quantile predictions per row (may cross).
2. **CQR (Romano, Patterson & Candès 2019)** on each symmetric pair. For each interval `(lo, hi) ∈ {(p2.5,p97.5),(p5,p95),(p10,p90),(p25,p75)}` at miscoverage `α ∈ {0.05, 0.10, 0.20, 0.50}`, compute on the calibration set the conformity score `E_i = max{ q̂_lo(x_i) − y_i , y_i − q̂_hi(x_i) }`, take the threshold `Q_(lo,hi)` as the `⌈(n_cal+1)(1−α)⌉`-th smallest `E_i`, and form `[q̂_lo − Q, q̂_hi + Q]`. The median (p50) is left un-shifted for the point forecast.
3. **Isotonic regression (last)** across the nine conformalized endpoints per row, as the final ordering guard.

**Calibration set (pinned, carried from v5.5):** the **final 60–90 days of each fold's training partition (~1,440–2,160 hourly rows)**, held out and disjoint from both proper-training and eval, refit per fold. The tradeoff is stated once: those rows leave proper-training — negligible against years of training data, and it keeps the conformity scores on the most-recent (most regime-relevant) data.

**Why CQR, and why isotonic is still last (verbatim).** For a **single already-ordered interval pair**, a nonnegative CQR threshold widens the pair and cannot invert its two endpoints; a negative threshold narrows it and can. That statement is only pairwise: the raw heads may already cross, and the four pair-specific thresholds can leave the **nine** post-CQR endpoints globally unordered for either threshold sign. Isotonic therefore remains the unconditional final ordering guard. **Coverage claim (v6.3 — three stages, stated precisely):** the finite-sample, distribution-free **per-interval marginal guarantee ≥ 1−α under exchangeability attaches to the post-CQR / pre-isotonic output** (stage 2). Isotonic is a monotone rearrangement applied last (stage 3) and is inactive only when all nine post-CQR endpoints are already non-decreasing. Whenever it moves an endpoint it perturbs the interval, so **the coverage of the final post-isotonic output (stage 3) is reported as empirical**, verified on the reliability diagram (§8.4) at all three stages (raw / post-CQR / final). Do **not** claim the formal guarantee for the final output, and do **not** claim joint/simultaneous coverage across the four intervals; both caveats are stated.

Stated in the final report verbatim, for interview defense:

> *CQR provides finite-sample marginal coverage guarantees under exchangeability. The walk-forward CV mildly violates exchangeability — the crisis regime is not exchangeable with the pre-crisis regime, and the solar-driven negative-price era is not exchangeable with either — so empirical coverage may diverge from nominal on regime-shift folds. This is documented in the reliability diagram (Section 8.4).*

That paragraph is the heart of the project's uncertainty story.

### 6.3 Why a single zonal target, not components

The PJM version defended a monolithic target against an SMP/MCC/MLC decomposition. DE-LU needs no such defense: the day-ahead clearing price is a **single zonal price by construction** — there is no congestion/loss decomposition to model separately at the bidding-zone level. The architectural simplicity is inherited from the market design; the section is retained so the interview answer exists: *"DE-LU is one coupled zonal price; decomposition modeling is a nodal-market question, not a bidding-zone question."* (Intra-zonal redispatch costs are a grid-operations quantity, not a component of the DA clearing price, and are out of scope.)

## 7. Benchmarks and Statistical Testing

LightGBM is compared against:

- **Similar-day naïve (headline comparator)** — the exact Lago et al. (2021) EPF convention: for Tue–Fri, forecast = same hour of the previous day; for Mon/Sat/Sun, forecast = same hour of the same weekday one week prior. Matching the published convention keeps the DM results comparable to the benchmark literature.
- **Seasonal-naïve 168h** — forecast = price 168 hours ago. Week-over-week seasonality baseline.
- **Ridge regression** with the same engineered feature catalog. Classical baseline; checks whether the boosted-tree complexity earns its place (and reuses the Month-0 OLS/normal-equation work).

Metrics: **MAE** on the median forecast, **mean pinball loss** across the nine quantiles, **empirical coverage** at the 50% / 80% / 95% nominal levels.

### 7.1 Diebold-Mariano tests (point and probabilistic)

*(Machinery carried verbatim from v5.5; comparator relabeled.)* Two DM tests are reported, both against the similar-day naïve:

**(a) Point accuracy — DM on median absolute error.** Loss differential on the daily median forecast.

**(b) Probabilistic skill — DM on the mean pinball-loss differential.** Per hour `h` and day `t`, per quantile `τ`, pinball loss `L_τ(t,h) = max(τ·(y−q̂_τ), (τ−1)·(y−q̂_τ))`; day-level model loss `L_model(t) = (1/24)Σ_h (1/9)Σ_τ L_τ(t,h)`; differential `d_t = L_model(t) − L_naive(t)`. One-sided DM `= mean(d)/sqrt(LRV(d)/N)`, **LRV via Newey–West** with lag truncation `⌊N^{1/3}⌋`; reject (model better) if DM < −1.645. Use the **multivariate, pooled-across-hours** daily-vector form as the headline; report per-hour DM only as a diagnostic. Averaging pinball across the nine levels is a discrete approximation to CRPS; explicit CRPS is optional. (On the two DST days and post-transition days the daily average runs over the actual local hour count; the UTC index makes this mechanical.)

**Pre-committed secondary gate (carried verbatim, fixed before any results exist):** the CP-2 pass/fail decision is primarily the one-sided DM on Fold 5; **if Fold-5 DM p ∈ [0.05, 0.15] but the pooled-across-folds daily-vector DM (same Newey–West LRV) is significant at p < 0.05, M2 passes and the report states both numbers.** A single 90-day window carries sampling variance a genuinely better model can fail on; pooling across the five three-regime folds removes that variance from the gate. Committing the rule now, before results, is what keeps it a decision rule rather than post-hoc selection.

The probabilistic DM (b) is the one aligned with the project's thesis — it tests whether the *intervals* beat persistence, not just the median.

### 7.2 Post-gate information benchmark (v6.3)

A controlled ablation that **quantifies the value of post-gate information without ever shipping it.** It runs **once, at M3/CP-3**, on the **strict-gate catalog frozen at CP-2** (§4.1):

- **Two variants only.** *Strict* = the frozen CP-2 champion runtime catalog (no delivery-day A69). *A69-augmented* = the same catalog **plus delivery-day A69 and its derivatives** (the A69-based residual load, `dunkelflaute_flag`, and any A69-based standardizations) — and nothing else different. Both retain the same proper-training-only preprocessing already present in the strict arm.
- **Everything else identical.** Same evaluation rows, same five pinned folds, same base features, same preprocessing, same seeds, same hyperparameter search space and tuning budget, same calibration windows. **Each variant is calibrated separately** through the identical CQR-then-isotonic protocol on its own calibration slice — so the comparison isolates the A69 information, not a calibration artifact.
- **Reported metrics (five, by name):** **MAE** (median forecast); **mean pinball loss** (nine quantiles); **empirical coverage** (50/80/95); **mean interval width** (50/80/95); and **calibration error** — defined as the **mean absolute nominal-minus-empirical coverage gap across the 50/80/95 levels** on the pooled eval (report both per-level gaps and their mean).
- **It is a benchmark, never the champion.** The A69-augmented model is not registered, not deployed, and cannot be promoted; A69 is never reclassified KFT. The result is a single honest reading in the report — *"post-gate VRE information is worth X % pinball loss / Y pp coverage"* — that makes the strict-gate cost explicit.

The benchmark is the audit-driven upgrade of the old as-archived posture: instead of quietly using post-gate data and disclosing it, v6.3 ships the feasible model and *measures* what feasibility costs.

## 8. Diagnostics and Explainability

*(Structure carried verbatim from v5.5; features and strata relabeled.)* These diagnostics are the heart of the portfolio artifact.

### 8.1 SHAP on the median model

- Global SHAP summary plot for the `p50` head **of the champion** on the validation tail; top-10 ranking; two dependence plots drawn from the **retained** champion features (**v6.3** — e.g. `gate_feasible_residual_load_proxy` × hour-of-day, and the retained headline proxy × a calendar/regime feature). The plots use only features actually retained at CP-2; if no domain proxy is retained, they fall back to the strongest retained features. The A69-based `dunkelflaute_flag` appears only in the §7.2 benchmark's SHAP, never the champion's.
- **Scoping (state explicitly):** SHAP on the p50 head explains the **central tendency**, not interval *width*. Interval width is driven by the inter-quantile spread and the CQR shift `Q`. If asked "what drives your uncertainty," the answer is the spread + `Q`, not the p50 SHAP. **v6.3:** SHAP *explains* the retained champion and may *name* a headline among already-retained features — it is **not** the incremental-value test (that is the M2/CP-2 matched ablation, §4.1). Trebbien et al. (2023, *Energy & AI*) — SHAP on a gradient-boosted model of German day-ahead prices — is the published methodological twin to cite.

### 9.4 Targeted invariant tests

A small set of **five mandatory property tests** asserts the invariants that must hold — deliberately **not** a regression matrix (Section 13). The first four carry forward; the fifth is the **v6.3 champion/benchmark schema separation**:

3. **UTC/DST lag alignment.** On the spring-forward (23-local-hour) and fall-back (25-local-hour) days, assert that a 24h/168h lag in UTC retrieves the correct row and that no hour is dropped or duplicated. Guards the §4.0 integer-lag corruption.
4. **15-minute aggregation integrity (extended at v6.1).** Assert the hourly price series is continuous across 2025-10-01 with no gap or duplicate; that every post-transition hourly price equals the mean of its four quarter-hours; **and that every hourly value aggregated from any PT15M feed (price post-transition; load/VRE feeds across the whole archive) is built from exactly four quarter-hours — no partial bins at chunk or pull-window edges** (the spike-surfaced boundary artifact). ### 9.5 Offline batch data/output-health report (v6.3)

A single **offline batch** report — **not** production monitoring, and deliberately **not** a model-performance/drift claim (no realized outcomes or labels exist at generation time, so it cannot speak to accuracy drift). Generated at **M4/CP-4**; its static rendering is verified at **CP-5**; it lives on the static Pages showcase as one more table + small figures.

- **Reference data, comparison window, as-of boundary, completeness (v6.3, pinned).** *Reference data* = the frozen M1 training rows (never recomputed). *Comparison window* = the **most recent complete 28-day span ending at the as-of boundary**. *As-of boundary* = the latest hour for which every required feed is fully populated; partial trailing hours are excluded. A feature-level comparison computes only if the 28-day window is **≥90% non-null**, else it emits **"insufficient data" (amber)**, never a spurious green.
- **Feature-aware distribution reference.** Deterministic calendar/regime columns are schema-checked but excluded from PSI. For continuous retained features, the frozen training reference is reweighted to the comparison window's joint **calendar-month × hour-of-week** mix; bins use deduplicated training-quantile edges (up to deciles). After binning, both continuous distributions receive **ε = 1e-6 in every bin and are renormalized** before `(p−q)·log(p/q)`, so zero-mass current or reference bins remain finite. Binary/categorical features and numeric features with **≤10 distinct training values** use category-frequency PSI instead: take the union of reference/current categories, apply the same ε smoothing, and renormalize. A nominally continuous feature with fewer than three usable bins falls back to category-frequency PSI **only if it has ≤10 distinct training values; otherwise it reports `insufficient data` (amber)**. This prevents expected seasonality, zero cells, duplicate bin edges, or an after-results fallback choice from masquerading as shift.
- **Checks (five):** (1) **freshness** — age of the latest row per feed vs. its documented expected-lag threshold; (2) **schema conformance** — exact column set, dtypes, UTC index continuity, and the 15-min full-bin invariant; (3) **selected-feature distribution shift** — feature-aware PSI on the **champion's actually-retained non-deterministic features** against the matched frozen reference above; (4) **interval width** — the deployed forecast's mean 50/80/95 width vs. the corresponding season/hour-matched training norm; (5) **quantile sanity** — zero crossings / monotonicity on the emitted forecast.
- **Outputs & acceptance.** A committed static table + small figures. PSI uses the pre-registered descriptive bands **<0.10 green, 0.10–0.25 amber, >0.25 red**; missing/ineligible comparisons are amber, never green. Freshness and schema thresholds are the feed contract; quantile crossings must equal zero; interval-width bands are frozen from the matched training reference before the current window is inspected. Acceptance = the report **generates and renders statically**, every check returns a value or explicit insufficient-data status, and all thresholds/reference metadata are rendered. It raises no alarms, schedules nothing, calls no service.
- **Hard cap:** ≤ ~8 h inside M4 (planning figure). **No new services, no live dashboard, no scheduled retraining, no AWS dependency** — a batch script emitting static assets, run by hand alongside the Monday refresh. *(If the parked DEC-AWS is ever ratified, this offline report is the baseline the AWS backbone operationalizes inside its manually-triggered pipeline — not net-new monitoring; see `aws-extension-spec_v1_1.md`.)*

## 11. Risk Register

### R-1 — Three-regime fragility
**Description.** Pre-crisis, crisis, and post-crisis price distributions are structurally distinct, with two opposing stress directions (high-price scarcity vs. negative-price abundance); training spans all three but the forecast serves the post-crisis regime. **Mitigations:** three-regime walk-forward CV with both stress folds (Section 5.1); `crisis_period` and `post_crisis` as first-class champion features (**v6.3:** `dunkelflaute_flag` is a §8.3 evaluation stratum / §7.2 benchmark feature, not a champion feature); per-regime and negative-price-stratum error analysis (Section 8.3); if coverage diverges on regime-shift folds (reliability diagram), the report names it as a regime-shift failure rather than glossing it. R-1 is the project's best interview narrative.

### R-2 — VRE-forecast vintage (the KFT trap) — resolved by strict-gate design at v6.3
**Description.** The day-ahead wind/solar forecast's regulatory publication deadline (18:00 D-1) post-dates the 12:00 CET gate, and **the spike observed directly (2026-06-12) that initial TP publication itself is post-gate** (A69/A01 absent at a 15:35 CEST D-1 pull, present after the 18:00 deadline window); the load forecast, by contrast, was empirically present post-10:00 — KFT confirmed. **Resolution (v6.3): the risk is eliminated for the champion runtime path by construction** — delivery-day A69 is not KFT, so it and its derivatives are **excluded from the champion runtime schema** (§4, §5.2, §9.4); the champion uses gate-feasible runtime features only (the proxies where the M2/CP-2 ablation retains them, else the strict-gate base), and the value of the post-gate information is *measured* by the §7.2 benchmark rather than quietly consumed. The proxy has one pre-registered historical-VRE lineage: aggregate A75 is used only as a proper-training fit target for frozen calendar-driven expectation coefficients (§4.1), never as a runtime input. **Residual concern, benchmark-only:** the archive-overwrite question is tracked by the committed snapshot/compare harness (`scripts/q2_vintage_probe.py`; first snapshot 2026-06-12 for delivery 2026-06-13); revision metadata is a confirmed dead end (constant `revisionNumber`; `createdDateTime` = pull wall-clock). A lagged-vintage proxy is not part of v6.3. R-2 shifts from *"a caveat we disclose"* to *"a runtime design boundary plus fit-lineage rule we enforce and test."*

### R-3 — Platform migration, API churn, and data quality
**Description.** Three concrete risks: (i) the ENTSO-E Transparency Platform was migrating through 2025–26 with community-reported endpoint timeouts, 403/429s, and response-format changes; (ii) known TP data-quality issues (missing TSO submissions, aggregation inconsistencies across Germany's four TSOs — 50Hertz, Amprion, TenneT DE, TransnetBW); (iii) token/access friction. **Spike observation (2026-06-12):** a clean session — no 403/429/migration symptoms; monitored, not closed. **Mitigations:** `entsoe-py` pinned (0.8.0) and upgraded deliberately; the **SFTP bulk path** as a second ingestion route (port-22 reachability untested from the spike sandbox — verify from the M3 only if needed; the chunked API is assessed sufficient for the M1 bulk size); **SMARD as cross-check and fallback-primary** (same Reg-543 data, CC BY, no auth) — the CP-1 reconciliation gate (ENTSO-E↔SMARD within €0.01/MWh on >99.9% of overlapping hours; **96/96 sample hours already within €0.01 at the spike**) converts the cross-check into a verified property.

### R-4 — 15-minute MTU transition
**Description.** The SDAC resolution change at delivery 2025-10-01 splits the target series into 24-value and 96-value eras; mishandled, it corrupts every lag and rolling feature after the boundary (the same failure class as DST, occurring once but permanently). **Spike findings (2026-06-12):** the boundary averaging path validated on samples (0 missing/0 duplicated hours; hourly = mean of 4 quarter-hours to 1e-9), **and** the feature feeds are PT15M across the whole archive — the aggregation discipline applies from day one, with the chunk-boundary partial-bin artifact as the live failure mode. **Mitigations:** the §0-item-4 averaging rule; §4.0's chunk-boundary bin-integrity requirement; `entsoe-py` 0.8.0 (date-switched resolution handling); the extended boundary invariant test (Section 9.4 item 4); Fold 5 deliberately evaluates entirely post-transition so the headline metric is measured on the series the product actually serves.

### R-5 — Spectral-EDA scope creep — added at v6.2
**Description.** The §4.2 frequency-domain work expands into filter design, wavelets/EMD, or frequency-domain forecasting, consuming capstone time for no hiring payoff. **Mitigations:** the block is capped at the four §4.2 artifacts and the 6–10h budget; wavelet/EMD/frequency-domain-forecasting work is explicitly out of scope (§4.2 guardrails); any new dependency beyond scipy/statsmodels is a scope violation by definition. **Trigger to escalate:** >10h elapsed or any proposed dependency beyond the named set — stop, ship the mandatory three figures as-is, and carry the residual to the orchestrator. **Owner:** learner.

## 13. What This Project Is NOT

Each omission is a deliberate scope decision, defended and interview-quotable. The v6.0 conversion **re-ratified** every v5.5 boundary that survives the market swap and added two new ones; v6.1 closes the single gas reopen condition on spike evidence. The only entries that died with PJM are those whose subject matter died with it (gas-fallback machinery, the alert-sourcing risk).

**No multi-day-ahead forecast.** *"The target is the next delivery day, because that's what the 12:00 CET day-ahead auction clears and it's the horizon at which every lagged feature is legitimately known at forecast time. A multi-day forecast made at the gate would have to consume prices that haven't cleared yet — a look-ahead leak. I'd rather ship a clean 24-hour forecast than a leaky multi-day one."*

**"Why isn't the wind/solar forecast a feature?" (strict-gate design, v6.3)** — *"Because it isn't known when the model runs. The day-ahead auction closes at 12:00; I verified at the project's data spike that ENTSO-E's day-ahead wind and solar forecast for the delivery day isn't published until the 15:35–18:00 window — after the gate. Most published EPF work uses the as-archived series anyway and discloses it; I went further and excluded it from the shipped model entirely, so the champion is feasible at 12:00 by construction, using gate-feasible proxies for the merit-order signal **where they earn their place in a pre-registered ablation** — and shipping without that feature if they don't. Then I quantified what I gave up: a controlled benchmark, identical except it adds the post-gate forecast, measures the cost in MAE, pinball loss, coverage, interval width and calibration error. So I ship an honest model and put a number on the value of information I chose not to use."*

**No quarter-hourly target (new, v6.0).** *"SDAC moved to 15-minute products in October 2025. I kept the hourly target end-to-end — averaging post-transition quarter-hours, which SDAC's own no-arbitrage constraint defines as the hourly equivalent — because a 7.5-year continuous hourly series with three regimes teaches the model more than nine months of quarter-hours, and because a 96-step target would have quadrupled the output dimension mid-project for no methodological gain. Quarter-hourly is the natural v2, and the boundary invariant test means the pipeline already handles the dual-resolution data correctly."*

**No standalone fuel-price layer (adjudicated closed, v6.1).** *"There is no free, daily, legally-redistributable TTF or THE gas series — Yahoo's exists but its terms prohibit redistribution, and I wasn't going to build a reproducible open repo on data I can't commit. I even probed SMARD's API at the project's de-risking spike: it exposes gas-fired generation volumes, not THE prices, so the pre-committed reopen condition closed on evidence. Residual load is the literature-standard German merit-order driver, so I tested a gate-feasible residual arm and kept it only if it earned stable out-of-sample value; if it did not, the strict-base champion and the A69 benchmark state that limitation explicitly."*

**No formal causal claim.** *"The model makes a predictive claim with calibrated uncertainty, not a causal one. The perturbation slider is a ceteris-paribus sensitivity probe — it shows how the model responds to a feature holding others fixed, which can be physically out-of-distribution, and the per-cell OOD flag makes that visible. SHAP attributes predictions, it doesn't establish causation. I deliberately stayed on the predictive side of that line."*

**No enterprise production pipeline or live trading layer.** *"Containerized marimo on Hugging Face Spaces backed by a DagsHub MLflow registry is portfolio-grade hosting, not production infrastructure — no CI/CD matrices, no scheduled retraining, no live per-visitor API ingestion, no trading gates, no scheduled/production drift monitoring. **(v6.3: there is one offline batch data/output-health report — freshness, schema conformance, selected-feature distribution shift, interval width, quantile sanity — but it makes no model-performance-drift claim, because no realized outcomes exist at generation time, and it schedules nothing and calls no service.)** I invested in interactive risk communication and transparent experiment tracking, which showcase DS judgment, rather than enterprise MLOps boilerplate. DVC was rejected as collaboration overhead the project doesn't need; live per-visitor pulls as brittleness that would break the demo."*

**No neural challenger — no NBEATSx, TFT, or transformers.** *"The peer-reviewed EPF evidence shows classical and tree-based baselines within a few percent of deep learning on hub- and zone-level day-ahead price; the marginal accuracy doesn't earn the complexity for a portfolio. LightGBM is the published-evidence-backed default — and the recent German-market XAI literature (Trebbien et al.) runs on exactly this model class. I'd rather demonstrate judgment than run a model horse-race. I can build and train networks — there's a shipped CNN in my portfolio — so this is a choice, not a limitation."*

**No LEAR / `epftoolbox` baseline.** *"LEAR is the canonical EPF baseline but a specialized library that obscures the DS skills the project demonstrates. Ridge on the same engineered catalog plus the similar-day naïve and the 168h naïve covers the same transparent-classical-baseline role — and the similar-day naïve follows Lago's exact convention, so the comparison stays anchored to the published benchmark."*

**No sequential conformal (EnbPI / SPCI) and no Giacomini-White.** *"CQR gives finite-sample marginal coverage under exchangeability — the property an interview reader is most likely to recognize and probe. EnbPI/SPCI handle non-exchangeability more aggressively but add conceptual surface that distracts from the core argument. The reliability diagram makes the exchangeability violation visible, and the limitations section names it."* **Pre-committed reopen condition (carried verbatim, the only one):** if the M3 reliability diagram shows empirical coverage on any regime stratum diverging from nominal by **more than 10 percentage points**, EnbPI returns as **remediation** — a documented comparison only; CQR remains the shipped calibration inside the champion `pyfunc`. Below that threshold the boundary stands as written.

**"Does isotonic break your CQR coverage guarantee?" (three-stage coverage, v6.3)** — *"It can, so I'm precise about it. CQR's finite-sample marginal guarantee attaches to the conformalized output, before the isotonic step. Isotonic is a monotone rearrangement I apply last only to kill residual quantile crossings; it does nothing **only when all post-CQR quantiles are already ordered**. The sign of a pair's conformal threshold is not enough to establish global ordering. Because isotonic can move interval edges whenever it acts, I report the final post-isotonic coverage as empirical and show all three stages — raw, post-CQR, final — on the reliability diagram, and I claim the formal guarantee only for the stage that actually has it."*

**No trading layer, battery arbitrage, or bid logic.** *"The deliverable is a forecast and a diagnostic report. Wrapping a forecast in a trading layer either needs real risk infrastructure or it's a toy — and a toy trading layer would undermine credibility. The forecast and its honestly-communicated uncertainty are the artifact."*

**No test matrix, no regression CI — but targeted invariant tests on a thin CI.** *"I wrote five focused property tests on the invariants that silently corrupt results if violated — quantile monotonicity after the CQR-plus-isotonic pipeline, closed-left rolling windows, UTC/DST lag alignment, aggregation integrity across the 15-minute market transition including full-bin completeness at chunk boundaries, and (v6.3) runtime-schema plus fit-lineage separation: delivery-day A69 and actual columns cannot enter champion prediction, while the VRE climatology may use A75 only inside proper training — and a thin GitHub Actions workflow runs them on every push. That's the right testing level for a single-developer portfolio: assert the properties that must hold and verify them automatically on every commit, not a regression matrix on a script that runs in five minutes. Still no scheduled retraining, no drift gates, no rollback machinery."*

**"How did you choose your seasonal features?"** — *"I didn't just assume 24h and weekly cycles — I confirmed them. A Welch periodogram of the detrended price series shows clear peaks at 24h, 168h and a 12h harmonic, which is exactly what the EPF literature reports (Weron 2014; Lago et al. 2018). That's the empirical justification for my hour-of-day and day-of-week features. It only motivates testing a seasonal residual-load candidate; the M2 ablation — not the price spectrum — decides whether that candidate belongs in the champion."*

**"How do you know your walk-forward folds respect the regime changes?"** — *"Change-point detection with `ruptures` defines the pre-crisis, crisis, and normalization boundaries; the folds are not mechanically cut at those dates. I deliberately pin five 90-day evaluation blocks so they sample every regime and both stress directions, while each training window still expands through time with a 24-hour embargo. Per-regime periodograms show why I should not collapse those results into one recent-tail story: seasonal amplitude and the noise floor differ by regime. That is an evaluation-coverage argument, not a claim that spectral analysis prevents leakage."*

**"You have a signal-processing background — where did it help here?"** — *"The spectral EDA. I used Welch's method rather than a raw periodogram because the series is long, noisy and non-stationary; averaging modified periodograms with a Hann window trades a little frequency resolution for much lower variance, which is what you want when you're confirming known cycles rather than hunting faint tones."*

**"Why is your portfolio link a static page and not the live demo?"** — *"Because a recruiter's first impression shouldn't depend on whether a free-tier container happens to be awake. The primary URL is a static export of the full report — CDN-served, loads in under three seconds, can't sleep — and the interactive demo is one labeled click deeper, with the possible ~30-second wake stated up front. Nothing methodological lives only in the live app: the interactivity is served from a precomputed lookup anyway. Decoupling the first impression from the runtime is the design decision."*
